Project Zymera · Team Blue · shared exploration

Connectivity-constrained cooperative coverage

A team of autonomous agents explores an unknown grid world under partial observability, communicating only over a limited-range, time-varying graph. Each agent acts on its own local sensing; the objective is scored at the mission level. The hard part is the coupling: the swarm must cover ground while keeping the comm graph connected, and pushing harder on one tends to cost the other.

Target. Reach ≥ 90% coverage and ≥ 90% connectivity on a 32×32 grid with 10 agents inside a 100-step budget — coverage and the comm graph held together at the same time, not traded off.

This gallery collects trained-policy rollouts across six experiment families — a method ladder on open floor, a team-size sweep, obstacle reward/barrier studies, crowded-world generalization, an evolution-strategies coexistence ladder, and a head-to-head of connectivity mechanisms (soft λ₂ penalty, Lagrangian, hard mask). Each card opens that family's own page; every page shares the same interactive viewer — press play to watch a rollout, scrub the slider, change speed.

Coverage figures shown on each run are the final fraction of free cells visited at the end of the 100-step rollout. Dashed teal edges mark the delivered comm graph (agents in range that step). Generated from data.js; rendering by viewer.js.
